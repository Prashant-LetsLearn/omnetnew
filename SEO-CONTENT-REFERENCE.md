### Q2: What is the difference between Managed IT, Fixed AMC, and On-Demand IT support?
The three engagement models cover different needs. **Managed IT Services** is a comprehensive
arrangement where the provider runs your IT end-to-end — proactive monitoring, patching,
helpdesk, vendor management, security, asset planning. Cost is usually per user, per month.
Best for companies that want IT as a non-issue. **Fixed-Cost AMC** is a defined-scope contract:
a published list of what is covered (laptops, printers, network, server) for a flat monthly or
annual fee, with a stated SLA. Best for offices that want predictable IT spend without full
outsourcing. **On-Demand IT Support** is pay-per-ticket or hourly — no contract, no commitment.
Best for freelancers, startups, or larger firms that already have an internal IT person but
need surge capacity. Omnet IT Solutions delivers all three models across Delhi NCR.

### Q4: What does L1, L2, and L3 IT support mean and which do I need?
L1, L2 and L3 are tiered support levels, organised by complexity. **L1 (Tier 1)** handles
common end-user issues — password resets, software install, basic Wi-Fi or printer trouble,
mailbox quota issues. Resolution is usually under 30 minutes via remote tools. **L2 (Tier 2)**
handles deeper technical work — server access issues, group-policy or Intune problems, Active
Directory or Microsoft 365 admin tasks, application errors that L1 cannot fix. **L3 (Tier 3)**
is for senior engineers — architecture decisions, security incident response, complex network
or virtualisation issues, vendor escalations to Microsoft, Cisco or Dell. A healthy IT support
contract gives you access to all three tiers under one roof, with internal escalation rather
than hand-offs to other companies. At Omnet IT Solutions, every Delhi NCR client account is

### Q8: What should a business in Delhi NCR look for in an IT AMC contract?
A solid IT AMC contract is specific, not vague. Insist on the following before you sign: (1) a
written **scope** that lists every covered device, location and software; (2) defined **SLAs**
with first-response and resolution targets per severity; (3) clarity on what is **billed extra**
(parts, after-hours, project work, third-party licences); (4) a documented **escalation matrix**
with named contacts and phone numbers; (5) **monthly reporting** with ticket volumes, SLA
performance and asset health; (6) a **named account manager** who knows your environment;
(7) **exit clauses** with reasonable notice and full handover of credentials and documentation.
Avoid "unlimited support" clauses without scope — they always mean unlimited best-effort, which
is no support. Omnet IT Solutions ships standard contracts that meet all seven criteria and are
negotiable to your specific needs. **To request our standard AMC template, call +91 97172

### Q10: What is the difference between a rack server and tower server — which is right for my office?
A **tower server** looks like a tall desktop PC and stands on the floor. It is quieter, runs
cooler in normal-room conditions, and does not require a server rack. Best for small offices
(under 20 staff), schools, retail back-offices and any environment without a dedicated server
room. A **rack server** is a chassis (typically 1U or 2U high) that mounts inside a 19-inch
rack. It packs more compute into less floor space, has hot-swappable drives and power supplies,
and is designed for 24×7 operation in a temperature-controlled room. Best for offices with more
than 30 staff, or environments where you anticipate growth, virtualisation or co-location at a
data centre. The decision usually comes down to: do you have a server room with cooling and a
rack? If yes, go rack. If no, tower is the practical choice. Omnet IT Solutions sells and
installs Dell PowerEdge, HP ProLiant and Lenovo ThinkSystem servers across Delhi NCR.
**Call +91 97172 70865 or email hello@omnetit.in for sizing advice.**

### Q11: How do I set up WiFi 6 for a 50–100 user office in Noida or Gurugram?
A reliable WiFi 6 deployment for a 50–100 user office follows a fixed sequence. (1) **Site
survey** — physical walkthrough with a heat-mapping tool to identify dead zones, interference
and material density (concrete walls, glass partitions). (2) **Capacity planning** — typically
one access point per 25–30 concurrent users for office workloads; more for video-call-heavy
floors. (3) **Equipment selection** — Cisco Meraki, Aruba Instant On, Ubiquiti UniFi and Ruckus
are all solid WiFi 6 options at different price points. (4) **VLAN design** — separate
corporate, guest, IoT and voice traffic on the controller. (5) **Cabling and PoE** — Cat6 or
Cat6A run from a PoE+ switch to each AP. (6) **Roll-out and validation** — install, configure,
then re-survey to confirm coverage. A typical 50-user office takes one to two days; 100 users
takes two to four days. Omnet IT Solutions handles the entire WiFi 6 deployment across Noida
and Gurugram. **Call +91 97172 70865 or email hello@omnetit.in for a site survey.**
